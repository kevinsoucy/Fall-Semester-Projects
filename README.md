# Fall-Semester-Projects

Summary of Logistic Regression to Compare the Availability of Affordable Housing Report

As part of the 2010 Census, the populations and properties of New Jersey, Tennessee, and Illinois were surveyed for information about property types, finances, and personal living arrangements. The survey gathered information about family and household income, property values, housing expenses, and other related questions.
In this report, we compare the overall quality and availability of low­income1 housing options between the three states New Jersey, Tennessee, and Illinois. We measure quality of low­income housing using three metrics; newness, affordability, and crowdedness. 

Summary of Clustering Hospitals to Predict Sales Report

In this report, I use orthopedic sales data and demographic information to cluster hospitals into market segments and ultimately predict potential sales gains for each hospital. The methods used include Principal Component Factor Analysis, Clustering Algorithms, and multivariate regression.  

Summary of Market Research Survey of Wine Preferences in Beijing and Hong Kong

In this report, I transform raw survey data from Excel into a usable SAS format, then analyze the differences in wine knowledge of implementers (sales personnel) and store owners in both Beijing and Hong Kong. The survey and analysis are conducted on behalf of a Wine Education and Certification provider.
